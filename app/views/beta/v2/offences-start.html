Jane Smith’s current alleged offences and previous convictions

Before you start

In this section, we’ll ask you the details below.

1. Current alleged offences

Any charges or accusations for which the applicant is currently being held in custody.

Do not add any historical allegations that have no connection with the charges the applicant is being held for.

2. Probation supervision

This only applies to applicants who are being supervised by probation.

If they are, we’ll ask if an OASys risk assessment has been done in the last two years and if it’s been recently updated.

3. Previous unspent convictions

These refer to any:

  • active community orders or sentences that the applicant is currently serving
  • past offences for which the applicant has been convicted and has already served the sentence for/li>

Most convictions or cautions become ‘spent’ after a specific amount of time.

Check if the applicant’s convictions or cautions are spent (opens in a new tab).

Add only previous unspent convictions that:

  • are relevant to the charges the applicant is being held for
  • relate to the following categories:
    • arson
    • domestic abuse
    • drugs
    • hate-related attitudes
    • stalking and harassment
    • violence
    • weapons and firearms

You should also include any previous convictions that might raise concerns towards other adults and children.

Do not add:

  • any convictions or cautions that classify as ‘spent’
  • unspent convictions that don’t relate to the relevant categories above

Sarah Jones' legal history:

Current alleged offences

She has just been brought into custody for a reported incident of shoplifting and is awaiting the bail hearing. She has been charged with Theft by the Crown Prosecution Service (CPS).

Previous unspent convictions

In 2024, she was charged with and found guilty of aggravated assault. As a consequence, she is serving a community order while on probation.

She also has an unspent conviction for possession of a prohibited knife for which she has completed a sentence for in 2021. This is still legally relevant (“unspent”).

Both previous unspent convictions are relevant to living in shared accommodation.
